Literature Survey

1. Healthcare Mobile Applications for Elderly

Research indicates that mobile health (mHealth) applications have shown significant potential in supporting elderly patients with chronic conditions. Studies by Chen et al. (2020) demonstrated that smartphone-based interventions can improve medication adherence by up to 40% in elderly populations when designed with appropriate user interface considerations.

2. Artificial Intelligence in Alzheimer's Care

Recent advances in AI-powered conversational agents have shown promise in cognitive assessment and therapeutic engagement. The work by Rodriguez-Martin et al. (2023) on multilingual AI assistants for dementia patients provided foundational insights for implementing culturally sensitive voice interactions in healthcare applications.

3. Geofencing and Location-Based Safety Systems

Location-based services for elderly safety have evolved significantly with GPS technology improvements. Research by Kim & Park (2022) on smart geofencing systems for dementia patients informed the design of our location tracking and alert mechanisms.

4. Computer Vision in Healthcare

Face recognition technology for patient identification and security has gained traction in healthcare settings. Studies on biometric authentication for elderly users by Thompson et al. (2023) highlighted the importance of accessible design and fallback authentication methods.

Key Research Insights:
  • Elderly-focused UI design requires larger touch targets (minimum 48dp) and high contrast ratios
  • Voice interaction is crucial for users with declining motor skills
  • Real-time family communication features significantly reduce caregiver anxiety
  • Multilingual support is essential for culturally diverse elderly populations

Design and Methodology

1. System Architecture

The application follows a modular architecture with two main components:

  • Patient App: Primary interface for elderly users with simplified navigation and accessibility features
  • CaretakerApp: Monitoring and management interface for family members and healthcare providers

2. Development Methodology

Agile development approach with 2-week sprints, focusing on:

  • Iterative user feedback integration
  • Continuous accessibility testing
  • Progressive feature enhancement
  • Regular security and performance validation

3. Technology Stack Selection

Frontend Technologies

  • Android Native (Java/Kotlin) for optimal performance
  • Material Design components with elderly-friendly modifications
  • Custom accessibility enhancements for TalkBack support

Backend and AI Integration

  • Firebase for real-time synchronization and authentication
  • Google Gemini API for conversational AI and cognitive assessment
  • TensorFlow Lite for on-device machine learning
  • Room database for local data persistence

4. Security and Privacy Framework

HIPAA-compliant data handling with AES-256 encryption, biometric authentication, and role-based access control to protect sensitive patient information.
